Why a tour beats a gallery

Photos pick the corners. A tour shows the floor.

  • 01

    The whole place is the pitch

    People walk the layout themselves, in the order they'd actually move through it — not a curated set of angles.

  • 02

    It pre-qualifies visitors

    The people who book a showing or a stay have already seen everything. Fewer wasted trips for everyone.

  • 03

    It never closes

    Your open house runs at 2 a.m. for a buyer two time zones away. The tour doesn't sleep and doesn't need staffing.

  • 04

    One asset, every channel

    The same link lives on your listing, your Google profile, your socials, and your site. Capture once, reuse forever.

Before you book

Questions worth asking.

How long does a shoot take?

Most homes and rentals are done in one to two hours on-site — figure roughly 1,500 square feet an hour. You don't need to be there the whole time; we just need access and the space tidied the way you'd want it seen.

How should I prep the space?

Treat it like a showing. Lights on, blinds open, clutter put away, beds made. Anything you'd hide from a buyer or a guest, hide before we arrive — the tour captures exactly what's there.

Can I put the tour on Airbnb and VRBO?

Yes. You get a hosted link that drops into Airbnb, VRBO, and your own direct-booking site, plus embed code for any page you control. The same tour works across all of them.

Do I get a floor plan?

The Lodge and Estate tiers include a schematic floor plan with measurements pulled from the scan. It's available as an add-on on the Cabin tier if you want it.

What's the turnaround?

48 hours standard, 24 hours on the Lodge tier. If you've got a listing going live or a hard deadline, say so when you book and we'll work to it.

Do you travel outside Cle Elum?

Easton through Ellensburg along the I-90 corridor is home turf, including Roslyn and the Suncadia communities. Farther out is no problem — travel beyond the corridor is just quoted per trip.
